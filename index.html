The Gateway

A Symphony of Nature and Urban Evolution

Majalengka Regency, historically known as a quiet agricultural hub, has transformed into a premier global destination without losing its authentic charm. Anchored by the Kertajati International Airport (BIJB), it is now more accessible than ever, inviting world travelers to discover its secrets.

Geographically uniquely positioned, Majalengka offers a dramatic transition from the sweltering northern coastal plains to the cool, misty highlands of the south. This topographical diversity creates a microclimate perfect for exotic agriculture, dense tropical forests, and a landscape carved by ancient volcanic activity. Whether you seek the adrenaline of summiting stratovolcanoes or the peace of meditating by crystal-clear spring lakes, the land provides.

Living Heritage

The Soul of Sundanese Culture

Our traditions are not confined to museums; they are lived, breathed, and celebrated daily in our villages and towns. Experience the vibrant tapestry of Majalengka's arts.

Tari Topeng Beber

A mesmerizing traditional mask dance that dates back centuries. The Tari Topeng of Beber village is distinct for its intricate wooden masks and the profound philosophical meaning behind each movement, representing the human life cycle from innocence to wisdom.

Jatiwangi Art Factory (JaF)

A globally recognized contemporary art collective situated in a former roof-tile factory. JaF transforms local clay into instruments, hosting the renowned Ceramic Music Festival and proving that rural communities can be epicenters of modern avant-garde art.

The Mystical Sintren

An ancient, mystical dance born from folklore. Accompanied by bamboo gamelan and chanting, a young maiden is put into a trance, magically changing costumes inside a closed bamboo cage. It is a rare, spiritual performance deeply tied to agricultural rituals.

Natural Wonders

Masterpieces of the Earth

From the highest peak in West Java to hidden subterranean rivers, explore the raw, unfiltered beauty of our ecosystems.

Mount Ciremai National Park

HikingWildlifeAltitude: 3078m

Standing as the roof of West Java, Mount Ciremai is a majestic stratovolcano offering some of the most challenging and rewarding trekking routes in Indonesia. The Apuy trail from Majalengka leads hikers through dense, mist-shrouded montane forests, home to the endangered Javan leopard and Javan surili, culminating in a breathtaking crater view at the summit.

Terasering Panyaweuyan

PhotographySightseeingArgapura

A mesmerizing expanse of neatly contoured onion fields clinging to the steep slopes of the Argapura hills. The geometric perfection created by local farmers over generations offers a surreal, emerald-green panorama. Arrive before dawn to witness the magical phenomenon of the "sea of clouds" rolling over the terraces as the sun rises.

Curug Muara Jaya

WaterfallNature WalkArgalingga

Hidden deep within a lush valley on the slopes of Ciremai lies this spectacular multi-tiered waterfall. The main drop plunges over 70 meters into a rocky basin, creating a permanent mist that nourishes the surrounding tropical flora. The well-maintained stone path leading to the falls offers a tranquil walk accompanied by the symphony of forest birds.

Situ Cipanten

LakeRecreationSindang

An incredibly serene lake fed by seven natural forest springs, resulting in water so clear it resembles liquid glass. Visitors can rent traditional wooden boats to glide over the submerged forest, observe the colorful koi fish swimming beneath, or enjoy the cool breeze under the shade of giant, ancient pine trees surrounding the banks.

Culinary Delights

A Taste of Authentic Majalengka

Our fertile volcanic soils and traditional recipes create a culinary landscape that is bold, fresh, and deeply satisfying.

Mangga Gedong Gincu

The pride of Majalengka's agriculture. This visually striking mango, characterized by its reddish-orange hue, offers an intoxicating aroma and an intensely sweet flavor profile unparalleled by other varieties. Best enjoyed fresh or as a premium local juice.

Jalakotek & Ampas Kecap

A quintessential street food experience. Jalakotek is a savory, chewy pastry filled with spiced tofu, typically served with a fiery chili sauce. Pair it with dishes seasoned using 'Ampas Kecap', a unique fermented soy byproduct that adds deep umami flavors to local stir-fries.

Legendary Artisanal Soy Sauce

Majalengka has a century-old tradition of producing premium sweet soy sauce (Kecap). Brewed using traditional clay pots, natural palm sugar, and black soybeans fermented for months, it is the secret ingredient behind the rich flavors of regional West Javanese cuisine.

Travel Smart

Essential Travel Tips

⛅

Best Time to Visit

To witness the greenest terraces at Panyaweuyan, visit between December and March. For hiking Mount Ciremai, the dry season from May to September offers the clearest skies and safest trails.

🚗

Getting Around

While public minivans (Angkot) are available, renting a scooter or a private car with a local driver is highly recommended to reach mountainous areas with steep and winding roads.

💰

Currency & Connectivity

Ensure you have sufficient IDR cash, as smaller vendors and remote tourism spots do not accept cards. 4G signal is generally strong, but may drop in deep valleys or high on Mount Ciremai.
