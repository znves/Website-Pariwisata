The Gateway of West Java

A Symphony of Nature and Urban Evolution

Majalengka Regency has rapidly transformed into a premier global destination without losing its authentic Sundanese charm. Anchored by the Kertajati International Airport, it is now more accessible than ever to the global traveler seeking uncharted territories.

Geographically uniquely positioned, Majalengka offers a dramatic transition from the sweltering northern coastal plains to the cool, misty highlands of the south. This topographical diversity creates a microclimate perfect for exotic agriculture, dense tropical forests, and a landscape carved by ancient volcanic activity. Historically, this region was a silent witness to the majestic Hindu-Buddhist kingdoms and the spread of Islam, leaving behind a rich tapestry of folklore, architecture, and resilient community values.

Whether you are an adrenaline junkie looking to conquer stratovolcanoes, an artist seeking inspiration in rural contemporary movements, or a weary soul needing the tranquil embrace of crystal-clear spring lakes, the land of Majalengka provides a sanctuary tailored to your spirit.

Living Heritage

The Soul of Sundanese Culture

Our traditions are not confined to museums; they are lived, breathed, and celebrated daily in our villages and towns. Experience the vibrant tapestry of Majalengka's unique arts and folklore.

Tari Topeng Beber

A mesmerizing traditional mask dance that dates back centuries. The Tari Topeng of Beber village is distinct for its intricate wooden masks and the profound philosophical meaning behind each movement, representing the human life cycle from innocence to absolute wisdom and spirituality.

Contemporary Art

Jatiwangi Art Factory

A globally recognized contemporary art collective situated in a former roof-tile factory. JaF transforms local clay into musical instruments, hosting the renowned Ceramic Music Festival and proving that rural communities can be powerful epicenters of modern avant-garde art.

The Mystical Sintren

An ancient, mystical dance born from coastal folklore. Accompanied by bamboo gamelan and chanting, a young maiden is put into a trance, magically changing costumes inside a closed bamboo cage. It is a rare, spiritual performance deeply tied to local agricultural rituals.

Gembyung Tradition

An Islamic-influenced traditional music ensemble originating from the spread of religion in the 15th century. Utilizing large frame drums and profound poetic chanting, Gembyung is typically performed during harvest festivals, circumcisions, and the commemoration of the Prophet's birthday.

Explore Nature

Magnificent Destinations

From the highest peak in West Java to hidden subterranean rivers, explore the raw, unfiltered beauty of our pristine ecosystems.

Most Popular

Terasering Panyaweuyan

Argapura, Majalengka

A mesmerizing expanse of neatly contoured onion fields clinging to the steep slopes of the Argapura hills. The geometric perfection created by local farmers over generations offers a surreal, emerald-green panorama. Arrive before dawn to witness the magical phenomenon of the sea of clouds.

Curug Muara Jaya

Argalingga, Majalengka

Hidden deep within a lush valley on the slopes of Ciremai lies this spectacular multi-tiered waterfall. The main drop plunges over 70 meters into a rocky basin, creating a permanent mist that nourishes the surrounding tropical flora. The well-maintained stone path offers a tranquil nature walk.

Extreme

Mount Ciremai

National Park Border

Standing as the roof of West Java at 3,078 meters, Mount Ciremai is a majestic stratovolcano offering some of the most challenging trekking routes in Indonesia. The Apuy trail from Majalengka leads hikers through dense, mist-shrouded montane forests teeming with endemic wildlife.

Situ Cipanten

Sindang, Majalengka

An incredibly serene lake fed by seven natural forest springs, resulting in water so clear it resembles liquid glass. Visitors can rent traditional wooden boats to glide over the submerged forest, observe the colorful koi fish swimming beneath, or enjoy the cool breeze under giant pine trees.

Cikadongdong River Tubing

Rajagaluh, Majalengka

Get your adrenaline pumping by navigating the crystal clear, rocky waters of the Cikadongdong river. Equipped with safety gear and guided by experienced locals, this tubing adventure offers a thrilling ride through pristine natural riverbanks and miniature rapids.

Paragliding Mount Panten

Munjul, Majalengka

Experience the wind city from the sky. Mount Panten offers optimal thermal currents for paragliding. Tandem flights are available for beginners, providing stunning aerial views of Majalengka's cityscape blending seamlessly with sprawling green rice paddies and volcanic silhouettes.

Talaga Herang

Sindangwangi, Majalengka

A cold, deep natural pool situated at the foot of Mount Ciremai, surrounded by dense forest and natural rock formations. The water is so transparent that the rocky bottom is clearly visible. It serves as a perfect retreat for swimming and escaping the tropical heat.

Curug Ibun Pelangi

Argapura, Majalengka

Often referred to as the Grand Canyon of Majalengka. This stunning location features a river flowing between high, curved rock walls. When the sunlight penetrates the canyon at specific angles, it creates a breathtaking permanent rainbow effect inside the cave-like structure.

Culinary Delights

A Taste of Authentic Majalengka

Our fertile volcanic soils and traditional centuries-old recipes create a culinary landscape that is bold, fresh, and deeply satisfying to the soul.

Mangga Gedong Gincu

The undisputed pride of Majalengka's agriculture. This visually striking mango, characterized by its reddish-orange skin resembling lipstick blush, offers an intoxicating aroma and an intensely sweet, slightly tangy flavor profile unparalleled by any other variety globally.

Jalakotek Traditional Snack

A quintessential street food experience. Jalakotek is a savory, chewy pastry made from tapioca flour, generously filled with spiced tofu and herbs. It is deep-fried to crispy perfection and typically consumed piping hot, dipped in a fiery local chili sauce.

Artisanal Sweet Soy Sauce

Majalengka harbors a century-old tradition of producing premium sweet soy sauce (Kecap). Brewed meticulously using traditional clay pots, natural palm sugar, and black soybeans fermented for months, it is the fundamental secret ingredient behind the rich flavors of West Javanese cuisine.

Legendary Sate Kalong

Despite its name translating to 'Bat Satay', this dish is entirely made of high-quality water buffalo meat. Sold exclusively at night, the meat is marinated in a complex blend of local spices and coconut milk before being grilled over charcoal, delivering an unforgettable tender and sweet bite.

Travel Smart

Essential Travel Tips

1

Best Time to Visit

To witness the greenest terraces at Panyaweuyan, visit between December and March. For hiking Mount Ciremai, the dry season from May to September offers the clearest skies.

2

Getting Around

While public minivans are available, renting a scooter or a private car with a local driver is highly recommended to safely reach mountainous areas with steep roads.

3

Currency Prep

Ensure you have sufficient Indonesian Rupiah (IDR) in cash. While the city center supports digital payments, remote tourism spots and local food stalls operate strictly on cash.
